Common Injuries

Abrasion: Open wound, road burn, or rug burn, skin scraped off
Avulsion: flap of tissue torn or pulled off.
Callus: thick painless area of skin (friction or pressure) use pumice stone to file off the skin
Incision: cut caused by clean sharp object
Laceration: irregular tear like wounds
Puncture wound: penetration of sharp object (tape object to body, stabalize and go to doctor)
Blister: fluid under skin bubble
Bites and Stings
- Paresthesia: numbness, tingling
- Ecchymosis: ruptured blood vessels, purple skin
- Systematic reaction: not just the injured part but whole body
- Anaphylactic shock: allergy from bee sting, drop in blood pressure and breathing
Contusion: BRUISE blood seeps into tissue (ICE ice compression elevation)

Strain: pulled muscle ICE
Myositis Ossificans: Bone replaces muscle tissue due to trauma. Calcium produced in muscle after a blow. Measure length, doctor
Tendonitis: Inflammation of a tendon cause of repeated stress, inflammation ICE.
Articulations (Joints) Ligaments hold bone to bone. Tendon: around joint connects muscle to tissue.
Sprain: Stretching or tearing of ligaments Symptoms: crepitation, deform, swelling
Crepitation: crackling or grating sound heard when damaged
Joint laxity: ROM of joints
Sprain Treatment: PRICE Treatment: protect (eg pressure), rest, ice (20 min on, 1 hour off), Compress, elevate (above heart)
Dislocation: separation of a joint
Subluxation: partial dislocation “felt as though it slipped out and then went back in.”
Dislocation Treatment: check for pulse, if none, call EMS. Comfortable position, ICE
Bursitis: inflammation of BURSA, fluid sac between joints, caused by repeated strain. PRICE
Synovitis: inflammation of ovial membrane
